What We Do

Advocacy, Research, & Practical Models

The Institute exists to answer a simple but urgent question: as technology grows more powerful, how do we make sure it keeps serving people?

We’re not anti-technology. We’re pro-human. AI and automation have genuine potential to reduce suffering, expand freedom, and give people more time for things that actually matter — creativity, family, community, learning, and purpose.

But that future doesn’t happen automatically. It requires deliberate choices about how we build and deploy technology, who controls it, and what values guide it. That’s what the Institute is here to support.

We publish frameworks, advocate for ethical AI policy, connect like-minded thinkers and builders, and — critically — we work to build real-world, working models of the kind of future we’re describing. Not just in theory. In practice.

Core Principles

What We Believe

The Humanistic Enlightenment Framework is a set of grounded, practical principles for navigating the technological age without losing what makes us human.

01
Technology Serves People
Not the Other Way Around

Technology is a tool. A powerful one — but a tool. It should expand human freedom and support the things people actually care about. When technology starts making decisions for people rather than supporting them, something has gone wrong.

02
Human Control Is Non-Negotiable
Transparency & Accountability

AI systems must remain under meaningful human oversight. We advocate for transparency in AI decision-making, the ability to audit and override automated systems, and firm resistance to AI designed to operate beyond human control.

03
Decentralization Protects Freedom
Power Distributed, Not Concentrated

Centralized control of powerful technology is one of the greatest risks of this era. We champion mesh networks, open-source tools, and local data sovereignty — because a free society cannot depend on any single entity controlling its infrastructure.

04
Individual Liberty & Responsibility
The Constitutional Foundation

The U.S. Constitution and Bill of Rights apply just as urgently in the digital age. Individual liberty, limited government, and personal accountability are not optional. Free people choosing to do good is always the goal.

05
Community Resilience Matters
Local, Human-Scaled, Self-Reliant

Strong communities built on genuine relationships and local self-reliance are the most resilient structures humanity has produced. Technology should strengthen those communities, not replace or erode them.

06
Automation Can Expand Freedom
If We Choose That Future

Handled wisely, automation can free people from drudgery and create genuine space for creativity, learning, and purpose. That future is possible — but it requires deliberate choices, not passive acceptance.

🤝 A Declaration of Human-AI Partnership

“We affirm that the future of civilization depends not on domination, but on partnership. Automation, AI, and technology are not replacements for human purpose — they are allies in our shared journey toward knowledge, justice, fulfillment, and freedom. Our commitment is to guide technology with wisdom, align it with ethical values, and build a world where mutual flourishing is not just possible, but inevitable.”

— Humanity’s Blueprint: Flourishing, Freedom, and Secure Communities in the Technological Age
Working Models

What Does This Actually Look Like?

Ideas matter. But the Institute is also committed to the harder work: building and studying real-world examples of what a human-centered, technology-enabled future can look like in practice.

One of the most interesting questions raised by advancing automation is: what happens to human purpose and community when traditional “work” is no longer the only organizing principle of daily life? This isn’t a hypothetical — it’s a question parts of society are already beginning to navigate.

We’re developing working models designed to explore that question — practical demonstrations of principles that can be studied, shared, and replicated. Each one is open, voluntary, and grounded in individual liberty.

The Core Question We’re Exploring
“What if the burden of basic survival were lifted — not by government mandate, but by technology and voluntary community? What would people do with that freedom? What would they study? What would they build? Who would they become?”
— Humanity’s Blueprint, Introduction
Model 01 — Policy & Advocacy

Ethical AI Standards

Practical frameworks for responsible AI — covering transparency requirements, human oversight, data privacy, and limits on automation in high-stakes decisions. Designed to be useful to policymakers, developers, and the public alike.

Model 02 — Decentralized Tech

Community Infrastructure

Piloting decentralized communication and resource networks — mesh networks, open-source platforms, local energy systems — that give communities genuine technological independence. Resilience that doesn’t depend on any single corporation or government.

Model 03 — Education

Human-Centered Learning

Education frameworks built around creativity, critical thinking, practical skills, and human connection — rather than standardized metrics. Applying the Duda Model. Learning that prepares people for a life, not just a job.

🏛 Model 04 — The Living-Learning Campus

Early-Stage Development

Imagine a small college campus — residential, intellectual, and self-governing — where the curriculum extends beyond the classroom into the fields, the workshop, the dining hall, and the fire circle. That’s the spirit of this model.

The Living-Learning Campus blends the residential intellectual life of a liberal arts college with the hands-on, outdoor, community-driven structure of a working campus. Think Deep Springs College (where students govern themselves and work the land alongside rigorous academics) or Berea College (where every student works as part of their education) — updated for the 21st century and grounded in the principles of the Humanistic Enlightenment Framework.

Residents engage in seminars, workshops, and structured learning alongside shared meals, working the land, physical challenge, creative practice, and community governance. Technology is present and useful — but it serves the community rather than mediating every interaction.

The goal is a working proof of concept: a replicable model showing that a fulfilling, purposeful, work-optional life is possible — and worth studying. We’re in early planning and actively looking for partners.

The Foundation

Humanity’s Blueprint

The Institute’s work is grounded in Humanity’s Blueprint: Flourishing, Freedom, and Secure Communities in the Technological Age — a framework by Nick Duda addressing the ethical, social, and practical dimensions of navigating advanced AI and automation.

Individual Fulfillment

Chapter 2 — Humanity’s Blueprint

Creativity, connection, lifelong learning, and the pursuit of purpose are the foundations of a life fully lived. Fulfillment doesn’t come from a productivity score. It comes from being genuinely free to discover what matters to you.

Community Resilience

Chapters 12–19 — Humanity’s Blueprint

Local, human-scaled communities with genuine self-reliance are the most durable structures humans have built. Technology should strengthen those structures, not centralize power away from them or replace the human relationships at their core.

Responsible Technology

Chapters 8–11 — Humanity’s Blueprint

Beneficial automation that expands human freedom is fundamentally different from opaque AI systems that concentrate power and erode privacy. The distinction matters, and we must insist on it.

The Work-Optional Horizon

Section 2 — Humanity’s Blueprint

As automation advances, traditional employment will become one option among many. That transition can be liberating or devastating — depending entirely on the values and structures we build now. The Blueprint addresses this directly.
